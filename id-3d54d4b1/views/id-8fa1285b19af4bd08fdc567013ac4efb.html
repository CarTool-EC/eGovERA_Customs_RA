OV-Special procedure [Motivation] ()
NARRATIVE: The OV-Special procedure [Motivation] view extends the Organisational view and provides a comprehensive framework for leveraging the special procedure in the context of customs and trade. This view is represented by a Diagram Model-group ABB named "Special procedure". The "Special procedure" is a Capability SBB, which is described as a special procedure (DBC). This capability is supported by several Business Service ABBs, including "Economic Operators Registry management", "Origin Registry management", "Customs declarations", "Classification of goods", "EU Customs information exchange", "Goods flow analysis and simulations", "Duties calculation and payment", and "Duties guarantee management". These services are all described as Digital Public Services, indicating their role in facilitating digital interactions in the customs and trade domain. The "Special procedure" capability also interacts with a range of Business Object ABBs, which represent different types of information that are relevant to the special procedure. These include "Duties calculation Information", "Economic Operators Information", "Threads and fraudulent actions Information", "Classification of goods Information", "Guarantee Information", "Origin of goods Information", "Internal logistic Information", "Binding Tariffs Information", "Workforce information", "Economic value Information", "Special Procedures Information", "Combined nomenclature", "Customs partners Information", "Imports information", and "Economic Operators Registration and Identification number (EORI) Information". These Business Object ABBs provide the necessary data and information for the execution of the "Special procedure" capability and the associated Business Service ABBs. The relationships between these elements indicate the flow of information and the dependencies between the different services and the capability. The motivation behind this view is to provide a clear and comprehensive representation of the special procedure in the customs and trade context, highlighting the key services and information elements involved. This can support strategic planning and decision-making, as well as operational efficiency and effectiveness.
