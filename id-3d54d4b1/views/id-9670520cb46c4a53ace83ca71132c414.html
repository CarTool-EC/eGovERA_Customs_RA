SV-Customs Form Structure [Motivation] ()
NARRATIVE: The SV-Customs Form Structure [Motivation] view extends Semantic view and provides a comprehensive framework for leveraging the structure and organization of customs forms and the information base related to customs. The view is composed of three main elements: "Customs Forms Structure", "Customs Information Base", and "Forms Structure". The "Customs Forms Structure" is a Grouping ABB that encompasses three Data Object SBBs: "BTI Application Form", "EUR.1 Movement Certificate", and "Single Administrative Document (SAD)". Each of these forms serves a specific purpose in the customs process, collecting and providing necessary information for customs authorities to make decisions regarding the classification, origin, and movement of goods. The "BTI Application Form" is used to request an official binding decision from customs authorities regarding the classification of specific goods under the Harmonized System (HS). The "EUR.1 Movement Certificate" certifies the origin of goods and claims preferential treatment in international trade. The "Single Administrative Document (SAD)" is a form used for customs declarations in the EU and other countries, aiming to reduce the administrative burden and increase the standardisation and harmonisation of data collected on trade. The "Customs Information Base" is a Diagram Model-group ABB that contains nine Business Object ABBs. These objects represent various types of information related to customs, including "Origin of goods Information", "Imports information", "Classification of goods Information", "Exports information", "Economic value Information", "Combined nomenclature", "Free circulation requirement information", "Economic Operators Registration and Identification number (EORI)", and "Economic Operators Information". Each of these objects provides crucial data for the customs process, contributing to the overall efficiency and effectiveness of customs operations. The "Forms Structure" is a Data Object ABB that enables the implementation of the layout and organization of the fields, labels, and controls on a form used to collect or display data. This object plays a crucial role in ensuring the usability and functionality of the customs forms. In terms of relations, the "Customs Forms Structure" and "Customs Information Base" are related in that the forms collect and provide the information represented by the Business Object ABBs in the information base. The "Forms Structure" is related to the "Customs Forms Structure" as it defines the layout and organization of the forms. The motivation behind this view is to provide a comprehensive and structured framework for managing and leveraging customs forms and related information, thereby enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of customs operations.
