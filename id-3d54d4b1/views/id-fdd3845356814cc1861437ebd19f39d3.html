OV-Expeditions controls [Motivation] ()
NARRATIVE: The OV-Expeditions controls [Motivation] view extends the Organisational view and provides a comprehensive framework for leveraging various elements related to expeditions controls. The view is primarily composed of a Diagram Model-group ABB named "Expeditions controls", which encapsulates a Capability SBB of the same name and a multitude of Business Service ABBs and Business Object ABBs. The Capability SBB, "Expeditions controls", represents the ability of the organization to manage and control expeditions. This capability is crucial for the organization's operations and is the central element of the view. The Business Service ABBs represent various digital public services related to expeditions controls. These include "Economic Operators Registry management", "Determination of the economic value of goods declared for importation", "Origin Registry management", "EU Customs Trader Portal", "Customs declarations", "Tariff Registry management", "Customs Decisions system", "Procedure Automation System", "Classification of goods", "Tariff Suspensions", "Goods flow analysis and simulations", "Intra-EU purchases and sales", "Duties calculation and payment", "Notification of Arrival, Presentation Notification and Temporary Storage", and "Duties guarantee management". Each of these services plays a specific role in the overall expeditions control process, contributing to the organization's ability to effectively manage and control expeditions. The Business Object ABBs represent various types of information that are crucial for the expeditions control process. These include "Origin of goods Information", "Internal logistic Information", "Binding Tariffs Information", "Workforce information", "Laboratories information", "Guarantee Information", "Classification of goods Information", "Threads and fraudulent actions Information", "Economic Operators Information", "Economic value Information", "Special Procedures Information", "Temporary storage Information", "Duties calculation Information", "Free circulation requirement information", "Combined nomenclature", "Customs partners Information", "Exports information", "Imports information", and "Economic Operators Registration and Identification number (EORI)". Each of these information types provides valuable insights that enable the organization to effectively manage and control expeditions. The relationships between these elements are not explicitly defined in the JSON, but it can be inferred that the Capability SBB is supported by the various Business Service ABBs, which in turn utilize the Business Object ABBs to perform their functions. The motivation behind this arrangement is to create a comprehensive and effective system for managing and controlling expeditions, leveraging digital public services and various types of information to achieve this goal.
