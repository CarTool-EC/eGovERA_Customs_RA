dct:titleNational legal act on custom cooperation between different member states
dct:descriptionThis regulation refers to the national laws enacted by individual member states of the European Union to implement and enforce the EU's customs cooperation regulations. These laws are designed to ensure the smooth operation of the EU's customs union, which involves the free movement of goods between member states and a common external tariff for goods entering the union. They cover areas such as customs procedures, duties and tariffs, and cooperation between national customs authorities.

skos:exampleFor instance, the UK's Customs (Cooperation with the European Union) Regulations 2020 were enacted to implement EU customs cooperation regulations in the UK. They provide for the sharing of information between UK and EU customs authorities, mutual assistance in customs matters, and the enforcement of EU customs regulations in the UK.
eira:concepteira:SolutionBuildingBlock
skos:noteThe regulation is crucial for the functioning of the EU's single market and customs union. It ensures that customs duties are applied uniformly across the union, prevents smuggling and fraud, and facilitates trade between member states. Each member state is responsible for implementing and enforcing these regulations within its own territory.
