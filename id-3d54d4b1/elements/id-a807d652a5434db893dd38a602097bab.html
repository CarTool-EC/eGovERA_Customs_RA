Customs Digital Business Capabilities Map ()
Definition: Customs Digital Business Capabilities Map (DBC) is a grouping that covers a range of elements related to the digital operations and procedures of customs. These elements include compliance requirements checks, customs cooperation, import and export procedures, guarantee, tariffs registry management, preservation of cargo information, cooperation between customs and postal operators, transit procedure, authorised economic operator accreditation, risk analysis, cooperation between customs laboratories, special procedure, laboratories management, MS' customs organization management, duties calculation and payment, and expeditions controls. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Digital Business Capabilities Map (DBC) is a comprehensive catalogue of elements that pertain to the digital functionalities and operations of customs. It includes a wide array of elements, each with a specific role and purpose in the customs digital business landscape. These elements range from compliance checks and cooperation between customs and other entities, to specific procedures such as import, export, transit, and special procedures. It also covers areas such as tariffs registry management, preservation of cargo information, risk analysis, and duties calculation and payment. The DBC is a crucial tool for understanding and managing the digital aspects of customs operations. Example: For instance, 'Compliance requirements checks' is an element that ensures all customs procedures and operations adhere to the necessary regulations and standards. 'Customs cooperation' refers to the collaboration between different customs entities to ensure efficient and effective operations. 'Import procedure' and 'Export procedure' are elements that detail the digital processes involved in importing and exporting goods. 'Risk analysis' is an element that involves assessing potential risks in customs operations to prevent any issues or disruptions.
