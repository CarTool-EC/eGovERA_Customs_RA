Customs Web Services Solutions ()
Definition: Customs Web Services Solutions ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of digital solutions and systems designed to streamline and facilitate customs procedures, trade facilitation, and regulatory compliance within the European Union. These solutions provide comprehensive information and tools for customs authorities, businesses, and traders to manage and facilitate the classification of goods, track goods in transit, validate EORI numbers, monitor customs transactions, and more. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The elements within the Customs Web Services Solutions ABB include CLASS - Classification Information System, EU Customs Trader Portal system, ECICS - European Customs Inventory of Chemical Substances, TRANSIT - Tracking goods in transit, EORI - Economic Operators Registration and Identification number, SAMANCTA - Sampling manual for customs taxation authorities, ILIADe - Inter Laboratory Inventory of Analytical Determination, EORI - validation open interface, MRN follow-up - Movement Reference Number, Surveillance System, TARIC - EU Customs Tariff, AEO - Authorised Economic Operator, CDS - Customs Decisions System, COPIS (COunterfeit and anti-Piracy Information System), and EBTI - European Binding Tariff Information. Each of these elements serves a specific purpose in the customs and trade process, providing essential information, tools, and services to facilitate efficient, secure, and compliant cross-border trade within the EU. Example: For instance, the CLASS - Classification Information System stores and organizes detailed information about various products and their corresponding customs codes, enabling accurate and consistent classification during import and export processes. The EU Customs Trader Portal system provides a single access point to the online systems for 'Authorised Economic Operators' (AEO), the 'European Binding Tariff Information' (EBTI), the Information Sheet on Special Procedures (INF), and Registered Exporter system (REX). The EORI - validation open interface allows external systems and stakeholders to validate the authenticity and correctness of EORI numbers in real-time, ensuring that customs declarations, trade documents, and other transactions involving economic operators are accurate and legitimate.
