Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ()
Definition: Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal and regulatory framework that governs the exchange of data and information between different systems, platforms, and applications within the customs environment. This includes laws, regulations, and guidelines that ensure seamless integration and interaction between different systems for efficient customs operations. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ABB is crucial in facilitating international trade. It ensures that different systems can effectively communicate and exchange data, thereby enhancing efficiency, reducing errors, and promoting transparency in customs operations. This legislation covers various aspects including data formats, communication protocols, security standards, and data integrity checks among others. It is applicable to all stakeholders involved in customs operations including customs authorities, traders, freight forwarders, and other government agencies. Example: An example of application of the Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI) ABB is in the implementation of a Single Window system. This system allows traders to submit all required documents and data for customs clearance through a single entry point. The legislation ensures that the Single Window system can seamlessly interact with other systems such as the traders' systems, freight forwarders' systems, and other government agencies' systems to facilitate efficient data exchange and processing.
