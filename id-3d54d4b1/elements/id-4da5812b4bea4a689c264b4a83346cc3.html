Customs Duties and Tariffs Management Enablers ()
Definition: Customs Duties and Tariffs Management Enablers ABB is a Grouping that covers the digital and automated management of customs tariffs and duties. This includes the systematic management of tariff registries and the accurate calculation and payment of customs duties. These services leverage digital technologies to enhance efficiency, reduce errors, ensure compliance, and support smooth trade operations within the customs environment. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Duties and Tariffs Management Enablers ABB includes two main elements: Tariffs Registry Management and Duties Calculation and Payment. Tariffs Registry Management refers to the digitalization and centralization of tariff schedules, rates, and updates within a digital registry or database. This service allows customs authorities to efficiently maintain, update, and disseminate tariff information, facilitating tariff classification and supporting customs compliance. Duties Calculation and Payment, on the other hand, refers to the automated and accurate determination and processing of customs duties and taxes owed on imported or exported goods. This service integrates with customs systems, financial institutions, and payment gateways to facilitate seamless payment processing, enabling importers or exporters to efficiently settle their customs obligations. Example: For instance, a customs authority could use the Tariffs Registry Management service to maintain and update its tariff schedules, ensuring accurate and consistent application of tariffs during customs processes. An importer or exporter could use the Duties Calculation and Payment service to calculate and pay the customs duties and taxes owed on their goods, expediting the release of their goods and ensuring compliance with customs regulations.
