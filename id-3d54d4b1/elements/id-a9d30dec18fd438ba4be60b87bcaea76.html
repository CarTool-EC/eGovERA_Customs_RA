Customs Legislation on Governance Interoperability (NNBI) ()
Definition: Customs Legislation on Governance Interoperability (NNBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal, regulatory, and policy framework that facilitates the exchange of information and cooperation between different governance systems in the context of customs operations. It encompasses the rules and procedures that ensure seamless interaction between various customs systems, promoting efficiency and effectiveness in customs administration. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The NNBI ABB is a critical component in the modernization of customs operations. It provides a structured approach to managing the interactions between different governance systems, reducing the potential for conflicts and inconsistencies. This includes the harmonization of data standards, the development of common protocols for information exchange, and the establishment of mechanisms for dispute resolution. The NNBI ABB also addresses issues related to data privacy and security, ensuring that the exchange of information complies with relevant laws and regulations. It is a dynamic framework, evolving in response to technological advancements and changes in the customs environment. Example: An example of the application of the NNBI ABB is the development of a common data model for the exchange of customs information between countries participating in a regional economic community. This involves the harmonization of data standards, the establishment of protocols for data exchange, and the implementation of measures to ensure data privacy and security. Another example is the establishment of a dispute resolution mechanism to address conflicts that may arise in the course of information exchange.
