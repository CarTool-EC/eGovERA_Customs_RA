dct:titleNational legal act on custom cooperation with private businesses
dct:descriptionThis regulation refers to the national laws implemented by each member state of the European Union, which aim to develop and enforce the EU's regulation on customs cooperation with private businesses. These laws are designed to facilitate trade, ensure the security of the supply chain, and protect the financial interests of the EU and its member states. They involve close cooperation between customs authorities and economic operators, including importers, exporters, manufacturers, carriers, and customs brokers.

skos:exampleFor instance, in Germany, the 'Zollverwaltungsgesetz' (Customs Administration Act) regulates the cooperation between the German customs authorities and private businesses. This includes provisions for the exchange of information, mutual assistance in customs matters, and the rights and obligations of economic operators. In France, similar provisions are found in the 'Code des douanes' (Customs Code).
eira:concepteira:SolutionBuildingBlock
skos:noteThe regulation is part of the EU's customs policy, which is a shared competence between the EU and its member states. It is implemented through national laws, which may vary from one member state to another. The regulation encourages private businesses to comply with customs rules and procedures, and to cooperate with customs authorities in the prevention, detection and investigation of customs infringements.
