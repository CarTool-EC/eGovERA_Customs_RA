dct:titleNational legal act on the processing of statistical data
dct:descriptionThe national regulation on the processing of statistical data in EU member states is a legal framework that governs the collection, processing, storage, and dissemination of statistical data. It is designed to ensure the accuracy, reliability, and confidentiality of statistical data, while also promoting transparency and accountability in statistical practices. The regulation is in line with the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the European Statistics Code of Practice.

skos:exampleFor instance, in the UK, the Office for National Statistics (ONS) is responsible for collecting and publishing statistics related to the economy, population, and society at national, regional, and local levels. The ONS must comply with the UK's Data Protection Act 2018, which implements the EU's GDPR, when processing statistical data. This includes obtaining explicit consent from data subjects, ensuring the accuracy of data, and implementing appropriate security measures to protect data.

skos:noteThe regulation provides a comprehensive framework for the processing of statistical data, with a focus on ensuring data protection and privacy. It sets out the responsibilities of data controllers and processors, the rights of data subjects, and the penalties for non-compliance. It also establishes procedures for data sharing and cooperation between national statistical authorities and other public bodies.
