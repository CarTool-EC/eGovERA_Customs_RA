dct:titleRegulation (EU) No 606/2013 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 12 June 2013 on mutual recognition of protection measures in civil matters
dct:descriptionRegulation (EU) No 606/2013 is a European Union regulation that establishes rules regarding the recognition and enforcement of protection measures in civil matters that are issued in one Member State and are requested to be recognized and enforced in another Member State. It aims to ensure the protection of a person in a Member State where the protection measure was not issued.
dct:publisherEUR-Lex
dct:sourcehttps://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32013R0606
skos:exampleFor instance, if a person in France obtains a restraining order against another individual due to threats or harassment, and then moves to Germany, this regulation ensures that the protection measure (the restraining order) is recognized and enforced in Germany as well.
eira:concepteira:SolutionBuildingBlock
skos:noteThe regulation applies to any judgment, court settlement or authentic instrument which orders a protection measure, regardless of the nature of the proceedings. It covers measures which aim to protect all victims and not only victims of gender-based violence. The regulation does not apply to public policy issues, revenue, customs or administrative matters, or the liability of the State for acts and omissions in the exercise of State authority.
