EU Customs Trader Portal ()
Definition: The EU Custom Trader Portal is an IT concept that refers to a digital platform used by traders to manage and streamline their customs declarations and other related processes within the European Union. Source: European Commission Source reference: https://ec.europa.eu/taxation_customs/business/calculation-customs-duties/what-is-common-customs-tariff/customs-procedures_en Additional information: The EU Custom Trader Portal is a comprehensive IT solution designed to facilitate trade within the European Union. It provides a centralized platform for traders to submit customs declarations, access information about tariffs, quotas, and other trade regulations, and manage their customs-related transactions. The portal is designed to streamline the customs process, reduce paperwork, and improve efficiency. It is part of the EU's broader efforts to digitize customs procedures and promote seamless trade across its member states. The portal is accessible to all traders operating within the EU, regardless of their size or the nature of their business. Example: Examples of application include: 1) A company importing goods from a non-EU country can use the portal to submit their customs declaration, calculate the applicable duties, and pay them online. 2) A freight forwarder can use the portal to manage multiple customs declarations on behalf of their clients. 3) A small business owner can use the portal to access information about the customs procedures and requirements for importing specific goods.
