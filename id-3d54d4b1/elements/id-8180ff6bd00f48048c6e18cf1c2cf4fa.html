Customs Privacy Data Policy Catalogue ()
Definition: Customs Privacy Data Policy Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers the elements related to the protection of sensitive and proprietary data within a business or organization. It includes measures and policies that ensure the confidentiality of business information, preventing unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Privacy Data Policy Catalogue ABB is a comprehensive collection of elements that pertain to the confidentiality of business information. This includes, but is not limited to, trade secrets, financial data, client lists, and marketing strategies. The catalogue emphasizes the importance of implementing appropriate security measures, access controls, non-disclosure agreements, and policies to maintain the confidentiality of business information. The aim is to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of such information, which could compromise the competitive advantage, reputation, or legal standing of the business. Example: An example of an element in the Customs Privacy Data Policy Catalogue ABB is 'Confidentiality of Business Information'. This element refers to the protection of sensitive and proprietary data belonging to a business or organization. It involves ensuring that confidential information remains secure and is accessible only to authorized individuals within the organization. Confidentiality safeguards aim to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of such information.
