Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ()
Definition: Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal, technical, and procedural elements that facilitate the exchange of information and data between different customs systems. It includes the laws, regulations, standards, and protocols that ensure seamless communication and data exchange between disparate customs systems, thereby promoting efficient customs operations and compliance with international trade laws. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The ENBI ABB is crucial in the context of international trade as it ensures that different customs systems can effectively communicate and exchange data. This is particularly important given the increasing digitalization of customs operations and the growing complexity of international trade laws. The ENBI ABB covers a wide range of elements, including legal provisions that govern data exchange between customs systems, technical standards that ensure the compatibility of different systems, and procedural guidelines that facilitate efficient data exchange. By promoting structural interoperability, the ENBI ABB contributes to the overall efficiency and effectiveness of customs operations. Example: An example of application of the ENBI ABB could be the implementation of a new data exchange protocol between two countries' customs systems. This would involve not only the technical aspects of establishing the data link, but also the legal and procedural elements, such as ensuring compliance with data protection laws and establishing procedures for data verification and validation.
