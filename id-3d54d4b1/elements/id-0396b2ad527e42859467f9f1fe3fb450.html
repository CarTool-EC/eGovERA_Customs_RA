Laboratories registry management ()
Definition: Laboratories Registry Management is an IT concept that involves the use of software or systems to manage, track, and organize laboratory data and resources. It includes the management of laboratory equipment, samples, results, personnel, and other related data. Source: Techopedia Source reference: https://www.techopedia.com/definition/24835/registry-management Additional information: Laboratories Registry Management is a crucial aspect of laboratory operations, especially in large-scale or high-throughput laboratories. It involves the use of IT systems to manage and track laboratory resources, including equipment, samples, results, and personnel. These systems can help to improve efficiency, reduce errors, and ensure compliance with regulatory standards. They can also facilitate data analysis and reporting, and support decision-making processes. The management system can be a standalone application or integrated with other laboratory information systems. It can also be cloud-based or installed on-premises, depending on the needs and preferences of the laboratory. Example: Examples of Laboratories Registry Management applications include LabWare LIMS (Laboratory Information Management System), which is a comprehensive solution for managing laboratory data and resources; LabVantage, a web-based system for managing laboratory workflows and data; and LabCollector, a modular software tool for managing laboratory samples and equipment. These systems can be used in a variety of laboratory settings, including research, clinical, and industrial laboratories.
