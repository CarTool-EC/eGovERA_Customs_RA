Computerised Transit ()
Definition: Computerised Transit is an IT concept that refers to the use of computer systems and software to manage, control, and optimize the movement and storage of goods, services, and related information from the point of origin to the point of consumption. It involves the integration of information flow, material handling, production, packaging, inventory, transportation, warehousing, and often security. Source: Investopedia Source reference: https://www.investopedia.com/terms/c/computerised-transit.asp Additional information: The concept of Computerised Transit is a key component of supply chain management and logistics. It is designed to automate and streamline the processes involved in the transit of goods, reducing human error, increasing efficiency, and improving overall supply chain performance. This includes everything from tracking shipments and managing inventory to scheduling deliveries and optimizing routes. The use of computerised transit systems can also provide valuable data and insights, helping businesses to make more informed decisions and improve their operations. Example: Examples of Computerised Transit applications include warehouse management systems, which use computerised systems to control and manage the storage and movement of materials within a warehouse; transportation management systems, which are used to plan, execute, and optimize the physical movements of goods; and inventory management systems, which use computerised systems to keep track of inventory levels, orders, sales, and deliveries.
