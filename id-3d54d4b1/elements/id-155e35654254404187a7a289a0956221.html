Special procedure ()
Definition: Special Procedure refers to the implementation of digital platforms and systems to streamline and automate the management of customs procedures that deviate from standard import/export processes. Source: Taxation and Customs Union Example: In Country XYZ, customs authorities have implemented a digital solution for managing special procedures, such as inward processing, within their customs operations. Traders can electronically submit applications for special procedures through an online platform, providing necessary documentation and details. The digital solution automates the processing and monitoring of these special procedures, enabling customs authorities to track the movement of goods, manage time limits, and generate electronic customs approvals. Traders can receive real-time updates on the status of their special procedure applications, reducing administrative burdens and expediting customs processes.
