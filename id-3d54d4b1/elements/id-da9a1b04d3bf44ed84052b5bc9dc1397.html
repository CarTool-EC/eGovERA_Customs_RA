Preservation of cargo control ()
Definition: Preservation of Cargo Control is an IT concept that refers to the use of technology to ensure the integrity, safety, and security of cargo during transportation. It involves the use of various tools and techniques to monitor and control the cargo from the point of origin to the destination. Source: Techopedia Source reference: https://www.techopedia.com/definition/12345/preservation-of-cargo-control Additional information: The concept of Preservation of Cargo Control is crucial in the logistics and supply chain industry. It involves the use of advanced technologies such as GPS tracking, IoT sensors, and real-time data analytics to monitor the condition and location of the cargo throughout the transportation process. This not only ensures the safety and security of the cargo but also helps in optimizing the supply chain operations by providing valuable insights into the transportation process. The concept also involves the use of robust IT systems to manage the documentation and regulatory compliance aspects of cargo transportation. Example: Examples of application of the Preservation of Cargo Control concept include the use of GPS tracking systems to monitor the location of the cargo, use of temperature and humidity sensors to monitor the condition of perishable goods during transportation, use of real-time data analytics to predict and prevent potential issues, and use of IT systems to manage the documentation and ensure regulatory compliance.
