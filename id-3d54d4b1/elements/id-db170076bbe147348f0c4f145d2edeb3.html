Internal Customs logistics management ()
Definition: Internal Customs Logistics Management is an IT concept that involves the use of technology to streamline and automate the process of managing customs logistics within an organization. It helps in reducing errors, improving efficiency, and ensuring compliance with customs regulations. Source: Techopedia Source reference: https://www.techopedia.com/definition/14365/logistics-information-system-lis Additional information: Internal Customs Logistics Management is a crucial aspect of supply chain management. It involves the use of IT systems to manage and control the flow of goods through customs, including the preparation of necessary documentation, calculation of duties and taxes, and ensuring compliance with all relevant customs regulations. This process can be complex and time-consuming, but with the right IT system, it can be significantly streamlined. The system can automate many of the tasks involved, reducing the risk of errors and speeding up the process. It can also provide real-time tracking of goods, giving businesses greater visibility and control over their supply chain. Example: Examples of application include the use of IT systems by import/export companies to manage their customs logistics. This could involve using a system to automatically generate the necessary documentation for a shipment, calculate the duties and taxes due, and submit the documentation to the relevant customs authorities. Another example could be a logistics company using an IT system to track the progress of goods through customs, providing real-time updates to their clients.
