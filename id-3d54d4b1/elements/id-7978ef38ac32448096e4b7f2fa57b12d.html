Customs Digital Public Services Catalogue ()
Definition: Customs Digital Public Services Catalogue ABB is a Grouping that covers a wide range of digital public services related to customs operations and management. These services include, but are not limited to, Economic Operators Registry management, EU Customs Trader Portal, Analysis Management, Customs logistics management, Centralised Clearance for Import, Determination of the economic value of goods declared for importation, Tariff Registry management, and many others. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Digital Public Services Catalogue ABB encompasses a comprehensive list of digital services that facilitate various customs operations. These services range from managing registries of economic operators and tariffs, to handling customs declarations and duties calculation. They also include digital platforms like the EU Customs Trader Portal, and systems for managing internal customs logistics. Furthermore, the catalogue includes services for compliance verification, partner authorization, and procedure automation. All these services aim to streamline and digitalize customs processes, making them more efficient and accessible. Example: An example of a service in the Customs Digital Public Services Catalogue is the 'Economic Operators Registry management'. This digital public service is responsible for managing the registry of economic operators. Another example is the 'EU Customs Trader Portal', a digital platform that provides traders with access to various EU customs services. 'Duties calculation and payment' is another service that facilitates the calculation and payment of customs duties.
