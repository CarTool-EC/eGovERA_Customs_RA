Transit procedure ()
Definition: Transit procedure refers to the implementation of digital platforms and systems to facilitate the smooth movement of goods under customs control from one customs territory to another. Source: Based on World Customs Organization (WCO) Example: A digital solution for transit procedures has been implemented between neighboring customs authorities to streamline cross-border trade. Traders submit electronic transit declarations through a centralized platform, enabling real-time data exchange and shared visibility. The digital solution automates risk assessments, simplifies document verification, and expedites customs clearances.
