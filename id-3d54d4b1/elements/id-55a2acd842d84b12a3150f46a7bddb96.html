dct:titleNational legal act on Classification, labelling and packaging of goods
dct:descriptionThis regulation, also known as the CLP Regulation, aligns the European Union system for classifying, labelling and packaging chemical substances and mixtures to the Globally Harmonised System (GHS). It aims to ensure a high level of protection of health and the environment, as well as the free movement of substances, mixtures and articles.

skos:exampleA chemical manufacturing company in the EU would need to ensure that any hazardous chemicals they produce are correctly classified according to the criteria set out in the regulation. They would also need to ensure that the chemicals are labelled with the correct hazard pictograms and risk and safety statements, and that the packaging used is suitable for hazardous substances or mixtures.
eira:concepteira:SolutionBuildingBlock
skos:noteThe regulation requires companies to classify, label and package their hazardous chemicals properly before placing them on the market. It introduces new classification criteria, warning pictograms and risk and safety statements. The regulation also sets rules for packaging of hazardous substances and mixtures.
