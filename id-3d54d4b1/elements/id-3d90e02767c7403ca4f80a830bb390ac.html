Customs Identification and Access Solutions ()
Definition: Customs Identification and Access Solutions ABB is a Grouping that covers a range of systems and services designed to manage and secure the digital identities of individuals, devices, and applications involved in EU customs processes. These solutions facilitate the management of authorisation assignments, the formalisation of electronic identification, and the tracking and recording of access to resources, facilities, and information. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Identification and Access Solutions ABB includes elements such as the Central Uniform User Management and Digital Signature System (UUMDS), which allows EU traders to manage authorisation assignments. It also includes the TAXUD Identity Management Service, an Application Service that manages and secures digital entities. The TAXUD Authorisation Service is another element, enabling processes to confirm the electronic identification of a natural or legal person, or the origin and integrity of data in electronic form. Lastly, the TAXUD Accounting Service is included, enabling the process of tracking and recording access to resources, facilities, and information. Example: For example, an EU trader could use the Central Uniform User Management and Digital Signature System (UUMDS) to delegate authorisation to a colleague. They could also use the TAXUD Identity Management Service to secure their digital identity, the TAXUD Authorisation Service to confirm their electronic identification, and the TAXUD Accounting Service to track and record their access to relevant resources.
