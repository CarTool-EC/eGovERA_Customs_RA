Economic Operator management and accreditation ()
Definition: Economic Operator Management and Accreditation is an IT concept that involves the use of technology to manage and accredit economic operators. This is often used in supply chain management and procurement processes to ensure that all economic operators meet the necessary standards and requirements. Source: European Commission Source reference: https://ec.europa.eu/growth/single-market/public-procurement/rules-implementation_en Additional information: Economic Operator Management and Accreditation involves the use of IT systems to manage and accredit economic operators. This includes managing the registration, qualification, and classification of economic operators, as well as monitoring their performance and compliance with relevant standards and regulations. The system can also be used to manage the accreditation process, which involves verifying that an economic operator meets certain standards and requirements. This can include checking their financial stability, technical capability, and compliance with environmental and social standards. The system can also provide a platform for communication and collaboration between economic operators and the organizations that manage and accredit them. Example: Examples of application include the European Single Procurement Document (ESPD) system used by the European Commission, which uses IT to manage and accredit economic operators involved in public procurement processes. Another example is the Supplier Qualification System (SQS) used by some companies to manage and accredit their suppliers.
