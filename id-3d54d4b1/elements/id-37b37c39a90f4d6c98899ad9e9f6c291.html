Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NNBI) ()
Definition: Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NNBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal, technical, and procedural aspects of customs operations that ensure seamless and efficient exchange of information between different systems, platforms, and applications. This includes laws, regulations, and standards that govern the interoperability of customs systems and processes. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NNBI) ABB is a comprehensive catalogue of elements that encompass the legal, technical, and procedural aspects of customs operations to ensure seamless and efficient exchange of information between different systems, platforms, and applications. This includes, but is not limited to, laws, regulations, standards, protocols, and guidelines that govern the interoperability of customs systems and processes. The aim of this catalogue is to provide a structured framework that facilitates the harmonization of customs operations, promotes efficiency, and enhances compliance with international customs standards and regulations. Example: Examples of application include the use of the NNBI ABB in the development of customs software systems that ensure seamless data exchange between different customs authorities, the application of NNBI ABB in the formulation of customs laws and regulations that promote interoperability, and the use of NNBI ABB in the design of customs procedures that facilitate efficient and seamless operations.
