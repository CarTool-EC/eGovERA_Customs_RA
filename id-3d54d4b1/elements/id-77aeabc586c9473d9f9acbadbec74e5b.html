Customs Information Base ()
Definition: Customs Information Base ABB is a Grouping that covers a wide range of elements related to customs procedures, regulations, and operations. It includes information on economic operators, customs partners, duties calculation, classification of goods, and more. This information base is crucial for the smooth functioning of customs operations and for ensuring compliance with international trade laws and regulations. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Customs Information Base ABB includes elements such as Economic Operators Registration and Identification number (EORI), free circulation requirement information, laboratories information, threats and fraudulent actions information, duties calculation information, imports information, combined nomenclature, workforce information, classification of goods information, temporary storage information, exports information, special procedures information, binding tariffs information, guarantee information, economic operators information, customs partners information, economic value information, internal logistic information, and origin of goods information. Each of these elements plays a critical role in customs operations, from identifying and registering economic operators to calculating duties and classifying goods. This information base is essential for ensuring that all customs procedures are carried out correctly and efficiently. Example: For instance, the 'Economic Operators Registration and Identification number (EORI)' element contains information about the EORI, which is a unique identification number that businesses need to import or export goods. The 'Free circulation requirement information' element provides details about the requirements for goods to move freely within a certain area. The 'Laboratories information' element includes data about the laboratories that test the goods for compliance with safety and quality standards. The 'Duties calculation Information' element contains information on how duties on goods are calculated.
